Budgie Hatching Guide: Successful Breeding in 18 Days

Budgie incubation takes place on average from the laying of the egg to the emergence of the chick. 18 days lasts. This process can be left largely to the mother's instincts given the right conditions; However, the correct interventions of the breeder significantly increase the success rate.

1. Preparing the Nest Box

from breeding season 2–3 weeks ago hang the nest box on the cage. Early insertion allows time for the couple to explore and accept the box.

Nest Box Dimensions

  • Base: At least 15×15 cm
  • Height: 20–25cm
  • Entrance hole: 4–5 cm diameter
  • Inner cavity: Make a 1-2 cm hole in the middle of the base to prevent the eggs from rolling.
💡 Tip

Pour a thin layer of wood shavings or coconut fiber onto the bottom of the nest. This material balances moisture and creates an ideal basis for claw development.

2. Ovulation Process

The female budgie usually 3–4 days after first egg hatching begins to incubate. Eggs are generally laid every day or every other day; Therefore, eggs in the same veil may be of different ages.

⚠️ Attention

If the female lays more than one egg per day or lays eggs outside the nest, review the inside cage conditions (light duration, temperature, noise).

3. Daily Monitoring of the Incubation Period

We can divide the 18-day incubation process into three main phases:

Days 1–5: Beginning of Embryonic Development

Cells within the egg begin to divide. During this period, when you hold the egg up to the light (candle), nothing is visible yet. The mother may have just started incubating.

Days 5–7: Candling Test

Hold the egg in the dark with a powerful flashlight. Red veins and a dark center appear in the fertilized egg. The unfertilized egg remains transparent—called “degenerate.”

Days 8–14: Rapid Development

The embryo grows rapidly. The inside of the egg appears almost completely dark. The mother rarely abandons the brood; Nutritional support is critical during this period.

Days 15–17: Pre-Departure

The baby sucks its beak into the air sac and moves into lung breathing. Soft sounds may be heard from the egg. Never break or crack the egg.

Day 18: Pipping

The baby breaks the shell from the inside with its egg tooth. Emergence may take 12–24 hours. Do not interfere — this process helps the puppy grow stronger.

4. Nutrition and Supplementary Foods

Nutrition of the mother and father is very important during the incubation period. After the baby emerges, the mother feeds it with the "crop milk" secreted by the gland; Therefore, the nutrients taken by the mother pass directly to the offspring.

Recommended

  • germinated seeds — source of protein and enzymes
  • egg food — amino acid support
  • Squid bone (cuttlebone) — calcium and shell health
  • Fresh vegetables: spinach, broccoli, carrots (small pieces)

Things to Avoid

  • Avocado, onion, garlic (toxic)
  • Excessive fruit (sugar load)
  • Moldy or stale food

5. Common Problems

Corrupt Egg

In the candling test, unfertilized eggs can be carefully removed after the 10th day. But if you're not sure, leave it to the mother — she will leave in time.

The Baby Who Couldn't Quit

If the egg does not hatch 24 hours after a crack forms in the shell and there is an extremely dry environment in the nest, the egg can be lightly moistened with warm water. Do this under the supervision of an experienced breeder.

Mother Wants to Abandon the Egg

It can occur due to noise around the cage, frequent handling, or stress. Move the cage to a quiet place and reduce the frequency of checking.

6. After the Baby Comes Out

newborn puppies blind and hairless is born as; They develop rapidly in the first 10 days. During this period:

  • nest box check daily — clean up any dead chicks or broken eggs
  • Replace sawdust if necessary (if moisture or dirt accumulates)
  • Puppies start to feather in 3-4 weeks
  • 6–7. Becomes independent in a week, can be placed in the main cage
